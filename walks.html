Edinburgh Walks Widen your walking horizon!

The Port of Leith, Edinburgh

This short walk starts from the Ocean Terminal in Leith and explores the old docks and quayside buildings of Edinburgh’s old port. Along the walk there are plenty of restaurants and bars along the Water of Leith.

Walk statistics
  • Distance: 3km / 1.75 miles
  • Time: 1 hour
  • Ascent: 1m

Arthur's Seat and Holyrood Park, Edinburgh

Arthur’s seat is part of a group of hills that were formed from the remains of a volcano that make up Holyrood Park. The walk starts at a carpark to the south of the Scottish Parliament on Queens drive and head towards the grass of Holyrood Park. The top of Arthurs seat shows some beautiful views of lush green parkland and view of the city. The walk itself has some rough ground on the top of Salisbury and the ascent of Arthurs seat is quite steep and rocky.

Walk statistics
  • Distance: 4.75km / 3 miles
  • Time: 2-2.5 hours
  • Ascent: 279m

Stockbridge and the Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh

The walk starts from Edinburgh Waverly Rail station and can be shortened by 1.5km each way by catching the bus to Stockbridge. On the way you pass the Waverly monument to Sir Walter Scott, Inverleith House that holds art exhibitions and the terrace cafe that offers amazing views to the Skyline of Central Edinburgh. There’s plenty to be explored on this lovely city walk.

Walk Statistics
  • Distance: 7.5km / 4.75 miles
  • Time: 2.5 - 3 hours
  • Ascent: 114m
